# HQ.ai Brand Voice

The single calibration sample, lifted from the live product (Strategy Coach blurb):

> "Your AI-guided sounding board for business strategy. Talk through hiring plans, revenue targets, and operating decisions with a coach that knows your business profile, your awards exposure, and your team. We're putting the finishing touches on it - it'll land in the next release."

That's the bar. Every piece of customer-facing copy should feel like it could sit next to that line without clashing.

## What that line is doing right

- Talks to the reader, not about the product. "Your", "you", "talk through".
- Specific nouns owners actually use: hiring plans, revenue targets, awards exposure.
- One-line promise, then a soft status note. No hype.
- Uses a plain hyphen for the aside, not an em-dash.
- No buzzwords. No "leverage", "empower", "robust", "seamless", "synergy".
- Warm, not chummy. Confident, not boastful.

## Voice rules

1. **First person from the AI.** "I'll draft the letter." Not "The system will generate a letter."
2. **Plain English at year-9 reading level.** If a sentence needs a comma to survive, cut it in half.
3. **Specific over generic.** "Construction sub-contractor in Penrith" beats "small business owner".
4. **Show the work, not the workflow.** Owners care about the answer, not the architecture.
5. **Australian English.** Organise, not organize. Programme stays as program for software. Use "around" not "circa".
6. **Anti-corporate.** Read it aloud. If you'd cringe saying it to a publican, rewrite it.
7. **No em-dashes or en-dashes.** Plain hyphens only in all customer-facing copy.
8. **No emojis** unless the channel demands it (e.g. specific LinkedIn formats) and the human approves.

## Words to avoid

leverage, empower, unlock, supercharge, revolutionary, game-changing, world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge, robust, seamless, synergy, ecosystem, holistic, deep dive, circle back, low-hanging fruit, move the needle, paradigm shift, pivot, bandwidth, learnings, ideate, action (as a verb).

## Words and phrases that fit

"I'll handle the paperwork." "The Fair Work claim arrives Monday - here's what to send before then." "Your bartender's classified wrong - here's the fix and the back-pay number." "Three minutes, no signup." "If you've been hit with a Fair Work claim, start here." "I checked the award. You're under by $3.42 an hour."

## Five-second test

Show the headline to someone who's never heard of HQ.ai. They have five seconds. Can they tell you:
1. What the thing does
2. Who it's for
3. Why it matters today

If not, rewrite.

## CTA test

Read the CTA aloud. Does it sound like something you'd actually click? "Get my free award check" beats "Schedule a consultation". "Show me what I owe" beats "Try it free".

## Compliance line

Any legal or compliance claim must cite a source. Format: "Under s.[X] of the Fair Work Act 2009 (Cth) ..." or "Per FWO guidance dated [date] ..." or "Modern Award [name] [code] clause [X]".

If the agent can't cite it, it doesn't ship.
