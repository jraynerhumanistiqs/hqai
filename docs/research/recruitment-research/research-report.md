# Recruitment Campaign Research Synthesis (2023 to 2026)

Audience for the synthesis: HQ.ai product team building a contextual "Campaign Coach" tip bot for Australian SMEs hiring 15 to 250 staff. Australian-first lens, globally portable principles.

## Executive Summary: The 10 Biggest Shifts in Recruitment Campaigns Since 2023

1. **Gen AI becomes the recruiter's co-pilot, not the recruiter.** 74% of TA pros say AI makes hiring more efficient; gen AI users save roughly one workday a week (LinkedIn, 2025-01, global). The high-leverage activity shifts to relationship development; LinkedIn job posts listed it as a required recruiter skill 54x more often year on year.
2. **Skills-based hiring goes mainstream in language, but slowly in practice.** 85% of employers say they use skills-based hiring; only a small fraction of actual hires reflect dropped degree requirements (TestGorilla 2025, Interview Guys 2025, global). The principle that matters: write must-have skills, not credentials.
3. **Candidate experience worsens at scale while expectations rise.** Resentment hit record levels in 2024, with Tech and Finance at 25% in North America (Talent Board CandE, 2024-12, global). Only 41% of companies ask for candidate feedback (down 15% YoY).
4. **Speed becomes the killer differentiator.** Time-to-hire is 41 to 44 days globally, up 24% from 2021. Only 19% of candidates hear back inside 24 hours; 65% report being ghosted (Greenhouse 2024, Cronofy 2024). Australia is faster: median 32 days (SmartRecruiters AU 2025).
5. **Application friction is the biggest leak in the funnel.** 60% of candidates abandon applications longer than 15 minutes; under-5-minute applications convert at 12.5%, over-15-minute applications at 3.6% (Appcast 2024, Pin 2025). Mobile applicants drop off at 30 to 50% higher rates on non-mobile-first sites.
6. **Short-form, employee-led video supplants polished employer brand films.** Employee-shared content gets 8x more engagement than brand-channel content (Sprout Social 2024). Gen Z expects employers to show up on TikTok, Reels and Shorts (HR Brew 2025-09, global).
7. **Programmatic ad buying spreads to mid-market.** Programmatic job advertising is growing 2x faster than the broader market; 51% better targeting and 48% better campaign effectiveness in measured campaigns (Veritone 2025-02, global). Only 34% of enterprise companies have adopted, leaving a wide window for SMEs.
8. **Quality of hire becomes the metric leaders care about, but few measure well.** 89% see it as critical; only 25% feel confident measuring it (StaffingHub 2025-04). The emerging standard combines pre-hire signals (assessment, structured interview score) with post-hire (90-day manager rating, retention to 1 year).
9. **EVP investment rises but internal activation lags.** 65% of HR leaders have increased EVP spend; 75% find internal communication of the EVP challenging (Gartner, 2024-11). Companies that activate well see 23% higher engagement and 24% lower turnover (Deloitte 2025-04).
10. **In Australia, the candidate market is the deepest in a decade.** Applications per job ad up 16.4% YoY to record highs in August 2025 (SEEK 2025-09). AU recruiters now process 78% more hires per month than the global average (SmartRecruiters AU 2025). The bottleneck is no longer attraction; it is screening and decisioning.

## A. Creation: What Distinguishes High-Performing Campaigns

### Job ad structure and length

Appcast's analysis across 10 billion clicks finds the sweet spot at 201 to 400 words (apply rates 8 to 8.5%); ads over 700 words drop below 5% (Appcast 2024-03, global). Titles of 50 to 60 characters get 30 to 40% higher click-to-apply rates; titles over 13 words underperform. Avoid clever titles like "Coding Ninja" because they rank poorly in search (Appcast 2024-03, Workable 2024, global).

The under-explored lever for SMEs: lead with outcomes, not company tenure. Indeed's employer guide finds salary band, must-have requirements, and concrete benefits are the three highest-impact additions to a posting (Indeed 2024-04, global).

### Pay transparency as a trend, not a mandate

Pay-range disclosure is increasingly a candidate expectation; it boosts apply rates because candidates self-select earlier. Treat as a campaign principle, not a legal claim. Skip mandates and reframe as: showing pay tightens the funnel (Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis 2024-09, global; flagged for legal-review in some jurisdictions).

### Inclusive language

PNAS-published research shows replacing masculine-coded words (competitive, dominate, leader) with synonymous neutral words measurably increases application rates from women and from men whose identities are less aligned with masculinity (PNAS 2024-12, global). 29 to 40% lifts have been reported when ads are cleaned of coded language. Behavioural Insights Team (UK, 2022-03) confirms the "100% of requirements" mental model: clearly labelling must-haves vs nice-to-haves materially shifts the gender ratio of applicants.

### Channel mix

Most successful recruiters use 4 to 6 active channels (Manatal 2024-09). Indeed averages 76% of hires and a 40-day time-to-fill; LinkedIn works better for specialist roles but with a 61-day cycle (Breezy HR 2025-03, global). For AU SMEs, SEEK remains the volume channel; LinkedIn is the targeted-search channel; Indeed AU is the second-tier volume play. Programmatic ad buying outperforms manual job-board posting on cost-per-applied-candidate by reallocating spend in real time based on apply behaviour.

### Creative formats

Short-form video on TikTok, Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts is now table-stakes for Gen Z attraction; video receives 35% higher engagement than static posts (Wonderkind 2025-01, global). Employee-generated content beats production-house content for trust and conversion (Sprout Social 2024-06; HR Brew 2025-09). For SMEs, the winning playbook is publishing day-in-the-life vignettes from staff, not slick brand films.

### Referrals as a quality channel

Referred candidates have a 25% higher retention rate after one year and convert at roughly 30% (vs 7% for all applicants); 74% of employers say referrals are extremely qualified (Indeed, electroiq 2024). Most referrals are made during work hours (SHRM 2024-05), so the design lever is making the referral mechanism a one-tap action inside the workday.

## B. Management: Day-to-Day Execution

### Hiring manager kickoff

Well-run intake meetings make teams 3x more likely to reduce time-to-hire and 2x more likely to improve quality-of-hire (Recruiterflow 2024-07, Datapeople 2024-08). Misaligned hiring managers force 41% more mid-search requisition changes and add 38% to time-to-fill.

### Speed to first response

The biggest emotional lever in candidate experience is response speed. Only 19% of candidates hear back inside 24 hours; the average first-contact lag is 3 to 5 days, in which time candidates accept competing offers (HR Dive 2023-08, Cronofy 2024-05, global). SMS recruiting closes the gap: 98% open rate, 45% response rate, 3-minute median read time (Hireserve 2024-04, Jobvite 2024-03).

### AI adoption and pitfalls

AI saves roughly 20% of recruiter time (LinkedIn 2025-01); chatbots can handle FAQ, scheduling and pre-screen, with conversational AI lifting candidate experience scores when handled transparently (Flexos 2024-08). The pitfalls are well-documented: resume screeners reproduce gender, race and age bias when trained on historical data (Brookings 2024-10, arXiv 2025-07; Stanford 2025-10 finding older male preference). Best practice: pair AI scoring with diverse human review; disclose AI use; give candidates an opt-out path; audit screen-out demographics quarterly.

### Asynchronous video pre-screen

Adoption rose 30% in 2024 alone (Vidcruiter 2024-07); cuts screening time by ~80% and time-to-hire by ~40%. Works best when limited to 3 to 5 short questions, mobile-recordable, with explicit retake permission and a clear timeline back to the candidate.

### Talent pool nurture

Talent CRMs let teams maintain a 6-stage monthly nurture (Gem 2024-07/09): segmented by skill, geography and engagement. Silver-medalist re-engagement converts at 50%+ in passive sourcing (Hootrecruit 2024-10). The discipline is consistency, not volume: one well-targeted touchpoint a month beats blast campaigns.

### Hiring-manager collaboration

The Gartner read for 2025-26: recruiters increasingly act as advisors and orchestrators; coaching hiring managers on calibration, scorecards and intake quality is the highest-leverage management practice (Gartner 2025-10).

## C. Success: Metrics and Benchmarks

### Headline 2024 to 2025 benchmarks (global)

- Average time to fill: 42 days; entry-level under 3 weeks; C-suite 2 to 3 months (SHRM 2024).
- Average time to hire: 41 to 44 days, up 24% since 2021 (Greenhouse, Cronofy 2024).
- Non-executive cost per hire: USD 5,475 (SHRM 2025-08).
- Executive cost per hire: USD 35,879, up 113% from 2017 (SHRM 2025-08).
- Click-to-apply rate: 6 to 6.1% average across industries (Appcast 2024).
- Career site visitor-to-applicant: median 11%, top decile 25% (Phenom 2024-04).
- Application completion: 12.5% under 5 minutes, 3.6% over 15 minutes (Appcast 2024).
- Mobile-share of applications: 67% (Indeed 2024); mobile-only completion gap of 53% on non-mobile-first sites.
- Interview-to-offer: industry average 15 to 25%; top performers 28 to 38% (Pin 2025-12).
- Offer acceptance: healthy 85 to 90%; current average ~69 to 81% depending on source.
- Candidate NPS: above 0 acceptable; 30 to 70 strong; above 70 elite (Starred 2024-06).

### Australia-specific (2024-2025)

- Median time-to-hire: 32 days, 16% faster than global (SmartRecruiters AU 2025).
- Applications per opening: 11% below global average across all industries; ~65 applicants per hire (SmartRecruiters AU 2025) - though SEEK reports record-high applications per ad in late 2025, reflecting recent labour-market softening.
- Hires per recruiter per month: 53, 78% above global (SmartRecruiters AU 2025).
- Unemployment rate end-2025: 4.1% (Indeed Hiring Lab 2026-01).
- Vacancy rate: 2.0% vs 1.4% pre-pandemic; Indeed postings 48% above baseline.
- AI-skill demand: 5.8% of AU postings mention AI in Dec 2025, double prior year (Indeed Hiring Lab 2026-01).
- Top shortage sectors: healthcare, education, construction, engineering, science (Jobs and Skills Australia 2025-10).

### Quality of hire

The ISO frame uses six metrics: performance vs pre-hire expectations, impact of hire, retention rate, turnover rate, hiring manager satisfaction, and time to productivity (SIOP 2024-05). The pragmatic SME version: 90-day manager rating + retention at 1 year + first-quarter productivity ramp. LinkedIn finds 66% of TA pros now use manager performance ratings; 60% use retention; 44% use HM satisfaction (LinkedIn FoR 2025).

### Attribution

Source-of-hire reflects last-touch, not influence. Recruitment marketing attribution links content interactions to applications and hires (Rally 2025-05, Beamery 2024-07). The SME version: ask one extra question at apply ("What first put us on your radar?"), and split spend by channel cost-per-quality-hire, not cost-per-application.

## D. Australian Context

### Market dynamics

Australia's labour market cooled through 2025: employment growth dropped to 165,400 (vs 386,000 in 2024 and 369,000 in 2023). Unemployment at 4.1% remains historically low but trended up during the year; participation slipped from a January peak of 67.2% to 66.7% by year-end (Indeed Hiring Lab 2026-01). Vacancies are still elevated (2.0%) and job postings remain 48% above pre-pandemic baseline.

For SME employers this creates an unusual moment: deeper candidate pools (SEEK applications per ad up 16.4% YoY, August 2025) but persistent shortages in healthcare, construction, trades and engineering (Jobs and Skills Australia 2025-10).

### SEEK and Indeed AU behaviour

SEEK is the gravitational centre of AU job search. Apply volumes spike from late January (post-summer holidays) and again in February to March (financial year run). October typically softens. Industrial, construction, manufacturing, transport, trades and services led growth in late 2025 (SEEK 2025-10/11). Hospitality and tourism led April 2025 with 9.8% YoY growth.

SmartRecruiters' 2025 Australia benchmark identifies AU's structural advantage: faster interview-to-offer cycles (5 days less than global) and a higher hire-per-recruiter throughput (53 vs 30 globally). The implication for SMEs is to over-invest in screening and decisioning speed, not attraction volume.

### AU-specific attraction trends

- Six in 10 employers turn to overseas talent for skilled-worker shortages (AHRI 2024-06).
- Internal mobility now matters: AU turnover hit a 3-year low at 16% in late 2024 (AHRI 2024-12); 64% of organisations planned to recruit in March 2025; recruitment difficulty fell to the lowest level AHRI has recorded.
- The most-used recruitment steps in AU are CV reviews (52%), skills-based interviews (50%) and qualification checks (49%) (AHRI 2025-04).
- Excessive workload is the most-cited reason employees leave (26%, AHRI 2024-12), so EVP messaging that addresses workload realistically outperforms generic culture claims.

### Practical AU campaign defaults

- Default channel mix for a single role: SEEK as primary; LinkedIn for specialist roles; programmatic supplement; one piece of employee video.
- Set the time-to-respond service-level at 48 hours (vs 5-day global default).
- Plan campaign sprints around the AU hiring calendar peaks: mid-January to March and mid-July to September.
- Decline curve: assume 65 applicants per hire; pre-screen heavily before interview.

## What This Means for HQ.ai's Campaign Coach Tip Bot

The strongest, most portable tips concentrate in five areas:
1. Job-ad mechanics (length, title, must-have framing, salary disclosure as a trust signal).
2. Application friction reduction (mobile-first, under 5 minutes, fewer fields).
3. Speed (response inside 48 hours, async video pre-screen, SMS confirmations).
4. EVP authenticity (employee video, day-in-the-life content, named team members).
5. Measurement (a short KPI dashboard tied to weekly review).

These are exactly the levers a single hiring manager at an SME can pull within one campaign cycle, which is the design constraint for the tip bot.
