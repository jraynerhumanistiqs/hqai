What's on each page

Eleven surfaces, top-to-bottom. Each one tells you what it's for, what's on it, and what we want you to look at.
0

Sign in screen

First page anyone visiting the site sees.

What's on it Email + password fields, a "create account" link, and a "send me a magic link" button (email-only sign in).
What we want from you Sign up with your work email. Does the page feel trustworthy? Are the choices (password vs magic link) clear?
1

Onboarding wizard

Three quick questions to set up your business.

What's on it Asks for business name, industry, state, headcount, and which Modern Award applies (or skip).
What we want from you Fill it in like a regular SME owner. Does the language feel plain? Does any question feel intrusive or unclear?
2

Dashboard home

The landing screen after you sign in.

What's on it A greeting, the left sidebar with every tool, and a list of recent work.
What we want from you Do you know what to click first? Is anything missing from the sidebar that you'd expect to find?
3

AI Advisor (chat)

An AI that answers Australian HR questions.

What's on it A chat box. Four starter topics (Employment Law, Recruitment, Pay/Performance, Redundancy) each with seed questions. Answers cite Fair Work / Awards.
What we want from you Ask 3-5 real questions. Does it sound like a calm advisor or a chatbot? Anything that's flat-out wrong - tell us the question.
4

AI Administrator (HR documents)

Generates contracts, letters and warnings.

What's on it A library of 33 templates (Letter of Offer, warnings, PIPs, terminations...). Pick one, fill a short form, generate. A Word-style editor opens so you can fine-tune the wording. One button to download a PDF.
What we want from you Try at least 5 different templates. Is the draft usable, or are you rewriting most of it?
5

CV Scoring Agent

Rank a stack of CVs against a role, then download.

What's on it Pick or build a scoring rubric for the role. Upload several CVs. Each gets a 1-5 band and a "next step" recommendation. You can override the AI, leave a note, compare side-by-side, and rename a candidate. Each scored candidate has three download options: CV Score Summary, Formatted & Branded CV, or Combine Both.
What we want from you Score 3-5 real CVs. Do the band and reasoning make sense? Try each of the three download options - which one would you send to a hiring manager?
6

Shortlist (video + phone screen)

Run first-round screens without a recruiter.

What's on it Set up a role with 3-5 interview questions. Share a single link - the candidate records short video answers. You also have a "phone screen" recorder for live calls. The AI scores each answer.
What we want from you Record a video answer yourself (use incognito). Try the phone screen recorder. Are the AI scores fair? Does any step feel like too many clicks?
7

Campaign Coach (job ads)

Writes a SEEK-ready job ad in a few minutes.

What's on it A short wizard. You answer light questions about the role; it writes an ad you can paste into SEEK or Workable. The final step gives you two exits: launch publicly across boards, or skip the ad and hand the role to the CV Scoring Agent for scoring only.
What we want from you Generate one ad for a real role. Does it sound like an SME you'd advise, or like a generic AI ad? Try the "Create Job in CV Scoring Agent (no ad)" button on the last step.
8

My Documents

Everything you've generated, grouped by type.

What's on it Collapsible categories (Candidate Resumes, Offers, Contracts, Employment Letters, Performance & Exits, Recruitment, CV Scoring Agent reports...). Click Edit on any row to open the same Word-style editor used by the AI Administrator, then download as PDF.
What we want from you After making 3-4 documents, come here. Edit one inline. Does the layout feel as polished as the All Templates page?
9

Settings

Business profile, logo, billing.

What's on it Edit your business details, upload a logo (lands in every generated PDF footer), set the human and AI advisor names, and manage the subscription. Test with Stripe card 4242 4242 4242 4242 - any future date, any CVC.
What we want from you Upload a real client logo and check it appears on the next document. Does $99 / $199 / $379 feel right for what you've seen?
10

$25 Letter of Offer (public page)

A pay-as-you-go entry for cold prospects.

What's on it A public page (no login). Sells a single Letter of Offer for $25. Open humanistiqs.ai/offer in an incognito window to see what a cold prospect would see.
What we want from you We're checking if you like the feel of this "non-member purchase" page - it's not functional yet, just aesthetic. Is the value clear in 6 seconds? Would you click "Get my letter for $25" if you saw it as an ad?

Read me What useful feedback looks like

The most valuable feedback is specific, repeatable, and tells us the "why". Here's the difference between feedback we can act on and feedback we can't.

✓ Useful

"On the AI Advisor, I asked about casual conversion and the answer ignored the 6-month rule. The new rules from August are missing." Specific page · specific question · specific gap.
"The Letter of Offer skipped the probation clause even though the role was 6 months casual." What you tried, what was wrong, why it matters.
"When I clicked Generate on a Termination Letter, the candidate name was in the recipient block but not in the body paragraphs." Reproducible. Useful.
"The Sidebar has Settings under Sign Out. As an owner I'd expect Settings near my profile photo." Tells us why your client would expect different.
"CV Scoring Agent rated a strong candidate at 'Maybe'. The rationale missed their 4 years in industry buried below the certifications block." Names the actual gap so we can fix the rubric.

✗ Not useful

"The AI doesn't sound right." We can't act. Sound right how?
"Templates need work." Which one? What was wrong?
"It feels clunky." Where exactly? On what action?
"I think we should add X feature." Skip unless it's the one feature blocking client use.
"The font size on the dashboard could be a bit bigger." Polish. Save it for launch. Tell us product problems first.

Most useful Five questions we want you to answer

These five things matter more than anything else. If you only have an hour, answer these.

1

Would a real SME owner click this?

Where does the language get too HR-jargon? Where does it talk down?

2

Does it save you 80%?

vs. how you currently do this work as a consultant. Be ruthless about where it saves nothing.

3

What's the one missing feature?

The single thing that would make you confident handing this to a client tomorrow.

4

Where do you mistrust the AI?

A specific clause, specific answer, or specific score that read wrong.

5

What would your clients break?

Walk a page as your hardest client. Where would they get stuck or give up?

Reporting How to send feedback

Feedback lives in the shared Google Sheet linked in the email. One row per piece of feedback - keep them small and specific so we can action them fast.

  1. Page name - which surface you were on (e.g. "AI Administrator", "Shortlist", "CV Scoring Agent").
  2. How serious - pick a category below (Show-stopper / Annoying / Polish).
  3. What you did and what happened - one or two lines. Drop a screenshot link or short Loom in the sheet if you can.
  4. What you expected instead - only if it's not obvious from what happened.

Show-stopper

Stops you doing the task

Can't sign in. Page won't load. Can't generate a doc. No way to recover.

Annoying

Slows you down or confuses

Hover state off, dropdown closes too fast, copy reads weirdly, missing labels.

Polish

Cosmetic

Spacing, alignment, colour. Nice-to-have, not need-to-have. Log it last.
